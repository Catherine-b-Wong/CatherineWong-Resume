I am Catherine Wong and I am currently a fourth-year student at the University of California, Riverside. I have been attending UCR for all four years and will be graduating in the spring of 2022 with a degree in biology.

Throughout my time in college, I have accomplished many professional experiences. In my first year, I got to work in the dynamic genome lab alongside Dr. Jamie Van Norman, plant cell and developmental biologist, in her own research experiment. This lab and many other labs that I have taken part in have given me valuable experience as I have learned how to organize records, dissection, pipetting, create and run PCRs, determine the structure of chemical properties, etc. In my second year, I got the opportunity to shadow an Obstetrician-Gynecologists all the way in Nebraska. In the hospital, I got to explore each department ranging from radiology to physical therapy to nurses, and really got to understand the daily routine of each. I had the great opportunity to stand in the operating room for 4 surgeries, which allowed me to fully experience the reality of surgery. I got to participate in doing rounds with the OBGYN doctor as he visited each patient to review their status and care plans.

In my third year, I worked as an intern at a private clinic for 6 months. I got to talk to the patients directly about their lab results, how to change small things about their lifestyle to improve their health, created and schedule appointments, refilled their prescriptions, and reminded them of their upcoming appointments. I also created and ordered labs for many of the patients through different diagnostic laboratory companies; through this, I got to learn a lot of medical terminologies that made it easier to understand the doctor and reports. In my fourth year, I took a two-month accelerated course to become an emergency medical technician back in San Francisco. This course was very rigorous as it dealt with basic anatomy and physiology that heavily emphasized the nervous system, cardiovascular, and respiratory systems. It was a combination of lectures and hands-on training that can range from handling a sprain to a patient being unconscious to a patient experiencing a stroke. This taught me how to handle situations on the spot and to critically think about all the possibilities that could be happening to the patient while choosing the correct steps to help the patient. Not only has this taught me critical thinking, but also how to work with others to form a functioning team and how to handle patients with proper care. I want to continue my personal journey in the medical field each year.
